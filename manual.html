ClipboardX User Guide

Show ClipboardX

Shortcut: ⌥V (Option + V)

Press ⌥V to open ClipboardX directly under your cursor. This provides quick access to your clipboard history without disrupting your workflow.

Flash Paste with ⌘V

Shortcut: ⌘V (Command + V) with additional navigation while holding ⌘

  1. Initiate Flash Paste: Press ⌘V and keep holding the ⌘ key.
  2. Navigate Items:
    • While holding ⌘, press V repeatedly to cycle through clipboard items.
    • Press a number key (e.g., 3) while holding ⌘ to select a specific item by its position.
    • Move your cursor over the desired item while holding ⌘.
  3. Paste Selected Item: Release the ⌘ key to paste the highlighted item.

This transforms your ⌘V into a visual, interactive paste shortcut—similar to ⌘Tab for app switching—while retaining the functionality of a regular paste command.

Smart Screen Record

Shortcut: ⌥R (Option + R)

  1. Start Recording: Hold down ⌥R and release it to instantly begin a screen recording.
  2. Stop Recording: Press ⌥R again to finish and save the recording.

This feature allows you to start and stop screen recordings effortlessly.

Instant Preview

Shortcut: Tab

While browsing your clipboard items, hold the Tab key to preview the content of the selected item instantly. This helps you quickly verify the content before pasting or using it.

Better Multiple Selection

Shortcut: Shift + Cursor Movement

Hold down the Shift key and move your cursor over the items you wish to select. The items will be highlighted for multiple selection, making it easy to select multiple clipboard items for batch actions.

Drop Mode

Shortcut: ⌥D (Option + D)

  1. Activate Drop Mode: Press ⌥D to open the Drop Mode window under your cursor.
  2. Drop Content: Click on an item to instantly insert its content at your cursor's location in the active application.

This feature lets you quickly drop content with a single click.

Easy Snapshot Area

Shortcut: ⌥S (Option + S)

Hold down ⌥S and drag your cursor to select the area you want to capture. Release to capture and save the snapshot to your clipboard. This provides a fast way to take area-specific screenshots.

Closing ClipboardX

Shortcut: Esc

Press the Esc key at any time to close ClipboardX and return to your work.
